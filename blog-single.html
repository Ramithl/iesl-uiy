Guidelines

You can mail your respective scanned copy of invention reports, the signed letter from the Department Head verifying the originality of the student's work as a PDF document.

  1. Engineering faculties of the University of Peradeniya, University of Moratuwa, University of Ruhuna, University of Sri Jayawardanapura, South Eastern University, University of Jaffna and Open University of Sri Lanka, will be informed of the contest and its rules and procedures each year by the IESL.
  2. You can participate as an individual/group project (Maximum 8 members per group.)
  3. To enter the competition, the student shall be a student member of IESL and an undergraduate of either of the above-mentioned faculties as of 10th July 2021.
  4. Students must submit their inventions to the Deputy Executive Secretary, IESL through a google form or website by 31st July 2021 with abstract, research plan and application form.
  5. A signed letter from the Department Head verifying the originality of the student's work will be required. The verification letter by the Head of the department should objectively set forth the extent to which the student exercised independence and originality and the part the advisor played. All the names of the inventors must be included in the verification letter.
  6. The preliminary competition will be held on 15th August 2021. Selected ten (10) inventions from each focus area will be eligible to participate in the UIY final in October 2021

Application Form

The online application forms should include the following information:

  1. Description of invention
  2. A brief note on the originality and novelty of the proposal comparing the invention with the existing products
  3. Description of current stage of development (Conceptual stage/ preliminary design stage/ Detailed Design stage/ Proof of concept stage/Working prototype stage)
  4. Details of team members including advisors
  5. (If any) Required Financial Assistance (Detailed Budget)

Preliminary competition

Need to submit

  1. A 3-minute video of the invention
  2. A brochure of the invention
  3. Respond to Q&A

Final competition

Need to submit

  1. A 3-minute video of the invention
  2. A brochure of the invention
  3. Deliver a presentation with a demonstration of the invention followed by a Q&A
